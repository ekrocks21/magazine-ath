There's a spot on top of Highlands where you can see everything at once.

Sandy Hook stretching north like a bent finger. The Navesink River cutting through the hills behind you. Manhattan twenty something miles away, shimmering on the horizon like something you half-remember from a dream. And below, the town of Highlands — small, weathered, holding on.

We started Above the Hook in September of 2025 because we kept finding stories in this place that nobody was telling. Not your regular stories. Not the beach traffic stories. The real ones. How a lighthouse built in 1764 has watched over every ship that ever sailed into New York Harbor. How a weapons testing facility on Sandy Hook helped win two world wars and then just … vanished into the dunes. How the hills you drive past on Route 36 are sixty-six million years old and still dissolving into the river below.

Stories like that don't need to be invented. They need to be found. And then they need to be told by someone who gives a damn about doing them justice.

That's what Above the Hook is. A place-based storytelling brand rooted in Highlands, Sandy Hook, and the northern Monmouth County coast. We tell these stories through drone photography, short films, historical deep dives, and now — this quarterly print you're holding.

Since launch, this community has grown to more than twenty-five thousand people across Instagram, YouTube, Facebook, and TikTok — almost entirely through word of mouth. Just people sharing stories about a place they love with other people who love it too. And now, millions of people know who we are.

This is the first issue of The Local Lookout. Inside you'll find the frozen Navesink, a look at what's coming to our YouTube channel, and a whole lot of the coastline that built us.

Welcome to the Hilltop. The view is worth the climb.

*— Eric and Mary*
