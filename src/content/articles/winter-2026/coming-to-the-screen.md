Sixty seconds is not enough.

We've spent the past five months telling stories about Sandy Hook and Highlands in one-minute reels. Some of those stories have reached hundreds of thousands of people. But every time we finish editing one of those reels, the same thought comes back: there is so much more here.

So we're going longer.

This February, Above the Hook Studios launched a cinematic documentary series on YouTube called *The Land Between Two Waters*.

Each episode is a standalone film — five to twelve minutes — exploring a single chapter of this coastline's history that most people have never heard.

A few episodes are live and even more are in production now:

---

**Episode 1: "First Light."** The origin story of Sandy Hook Lighthouse — the oldest working lighthouse in America, built in 1764, and the first light immigrants saw when they arrived in New York Harbor. A film about what it means to be the thing that guides people home.

**Episode 2: "Proving Ground."** The military history of Sandy Hook. From Revolutionary War gun batteries to Cold War Nike missile silos, a weapons testing facility operated on this sand spit for a hundred and seventy-five years. Then it was handed to the National Park Service, and most people forgot it ever existed.

**Episode 3: "Shifting Sands."** How a mile-thick glacier, an unstoppable ocean current, and sixty-six-million-year-old cliffs conspired to build one of the strangest landscapes on the Eastern Seaboard. A geology film disguised as a love letter.

---

Beyond the documentary series, we're developing a larger creative project called *Americana* — a period film set in the Highlands that will bring the history we've been researching to life through narrative storytelling. It's early. It's ambitious. But every great film starts with someone falling in love with a place and refusing to let its stories disappear.

The sixty-second version got your attention. The full version will keep it.
