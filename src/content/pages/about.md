# About The Local Lookout

The Local Lookout is the quarterly print magazine of Above the Hook. It is
published four times a year from Highlands, New Jersey, and covers a stretch
of coast that the rest of the state sometimes forgets it has.

Sandy Hook. The Highlands. The Navesink and Shrewsbury rivers. Raritan Bay.
The outer edge of the New York Harbor. The water between.

## What we're doing here

Most of the coast's history has been written down in pieces, by people who
were passing through. Dutch first mates. Quaker surveyors. Nineteenth-century
harbor pilots. Army engineers. The people who live here now inherit those
fragments, tell them to each other, and lose them faster than anyone realizes.

The magazine is an attempt to gather the pieces into something durable. The
same way a print issue is durable — paper, ink, spine, a thing you can set
on a shelf and find again in twenty years.

Each issue carries features on the history of this coastline, profiles of
the people who work it, essays on what the water does and has done, and
observation of the present — the ospreys returning, the ferries waking,
the particular light off Sandy Hook in late September.

## Who we are

Above the Hook is Eric and Mary's coastal storytelling brand. They live on
Goat Hill in Highlands, where the kitchen window looks down at the Navesink
and the kettle gets a great deal of use. Eric writes most of the magazine.
Mary shapes it. Contributors drift in and out as stories call for them.

The rest of Above the Hook lives at
[abovethehook.com](https://abovethehook.com) — the 24/7 harbor cam at
[harbor.abovethehook.com](https://harbor.abovethehook.com), the frequent
editorial blog at [blog.abovethehook.com](https://blog.abovethehook.com),
and the documentary film work published on
[YouTube](https://youtube.com/@abovethehookstudios). The magazine is the
slowest of them, and the one that is meant to last longest.

## The magazine itself

Set in Fraunces, Source Serif 4, and Inter. Printed on uncoated stock.
Approximately eighty pages per issue. Shipped to subscribers in time with
the season it covers.

Digital editions of each issue are available at this site.
