Summer on this coast is not a season. It is a population event. The bluefish
return to Sea Bright. The Sandy Hook lot fills by nine. The Parkway, south of
Exit 117, becomes a kind of slow mechanical river. Issue No. 03 watches it
happen.

This issue carries a feature on the last pound-net crew working the Navesink,
a visit inside the Twin Lights summer keeper logs, a story about what the
dunes at Sandy Hook remember from 1944, and a quiet afternoon on the bluefish
boats out of Belford. The summer here has always been loud. The magazine is
the thing that keeps a slower record of it.

Publishing early July 2026.
