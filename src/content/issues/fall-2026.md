Fall is when the peninsula quiets. The beach lots empty out, the parkway
unclenches, and the light goes lateral and gold across the dune grass.
Issue No. 04 walks the tail end of the warm season down the spit to the
tower that has run this coast since 1764.

Planned for this issue: a profile of the Sandy Hook Lighthouse keepers
who ran the light by hand for a hundred and fifty years, a fall-migration
piece on the hawks that use the dune line as a corridor, a visit to the
last charter captains still running out of Atlantic Highlands after Labor
Day, and what the fall striped-bass run looks like from a kayak in
November.

Anticipated for late December 2026.
