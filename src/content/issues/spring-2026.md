Spring arrives on the Shrewsbury the way it always does — the ospreys first,
then the gannets still passing north in diagonal lines offshore, then the
striped bass pushing up behind the herring, then the horseshoe crabs coming
in on the full moon tide. Issue No. 02 listens for all of them.

This issue carries a feature on Robert Juet, the English first mate who in
September of 1609 wrote the first known description of these Highlands in
any European language. It carries a story about Richard Hartshorne, who
bought Sandy Hook in 1680 for thirteen shillings, and what he and the
Lenape signatories each believed was being purchased. Jay Cosgrove talks
about four generations at Bahrs.

Spring, in a coastal town, is a kind of homecoming. The magazine's name
this quarter is a promise: Return.
